2. Problem Analysis

Deep dive into patient problems, doctor challenges, and market gaps

Problem Flow Diagram

Patient Problems

Multiple Medicines

Patients juggle 3-7 different homeopathy medicines simultaneously, each with different schedules, making it extremely difficult to track.

Similar Bottles

Homeopathy bottles look nearly identical with small labels. Patients frequently confuse medicines, leading to wrong intake.

Confusing Dosage Instructions

Complex instructions like "3 drops before food, 2 times daily" or "weekly dose on Sunday morning" are easily forgotten or misunderstood.

Missed Doses

Studies show 50-70% of patients miss doses, significantly affecting treatment effectiveness and recovery timelines.

Doctor Problems

Patients Not Following Prescriptions

Doctors have no visibility into whether patients are taking medicines correctly, leading to ineffective treatments and repeat consultations.

Frequent WhatsApp Clarification Messages

Doctors spend 30-60 minutes daily answering patient queries about medicine schedules, dosages, and timings via WhatsApp.

Lack of Adherence Tracking

No digital tools exist to track patient adherence. Doctors rely on patient self-reporting, which is often inaccurate.

No Digital Prescription Tools

Most homeopathy doctors still use paper prescriptions. No specialized software exists for homeopathy workflows (potency tracking, remedy selection).

Market Gap

Generic medicine reminder apps exist (Medisafe, MyTherapy), but none are specialized for homeopathy workflows:

  • • No potency tracking (30C, 200C, 1M)
  • • No food-related reminders (before/after food)
  • • No doctor-patient integration
  • • No visual medicine identification for similar bottles
  • • No homeopathy-specific prescription templates

This creates a significant opportunity for a specialized platform.
